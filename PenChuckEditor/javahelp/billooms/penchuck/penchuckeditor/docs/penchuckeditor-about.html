About PenChuck Editor

The PenChuck editor window has a control panel for adjusting the drawing parameters, and a display panel for viewing the resulting drawn pattern.

By right-clicking in the top of the window, you can un-dock the window and place it anywhere on your screen. You can also choose to maximize, minimize, etc. To restore the original window locations, go to the menu bar under "Window" and select "Reset Window".

Controls:

Pen Position

Pen Position defines location of a pen relative to the center of the spindle. A positive number means moving the pen forward (i.e. toward you as you stand in a normal position in front of the lathe). A negative number means moving the pen back behind the center of the spingle. You can change the pen position by entering a number directly in the text window, or you can change the position of the slider.

Pen Width

This defines the width (in pixels) of the drawn pen trace. You can change the pen width by entering a number directly in the text window, or you can change the position of the slider. You can't have a pen width less than 1 pixel.

Color

This sets color of the next line drawn in the display window.

Draw

Draw a trace in the display window using the current rosette at the given position of the pen.

Undo

Remove the last trace drawn in the display window.

Clear

Clear all traces from the display window.

Display Window:

The size of the drawn traces will adapt to the size of the window.
